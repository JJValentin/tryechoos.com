PART 1: WHAT ECHOOS ACTUALLY IS
========================================


EchoOS is a persistent, autonomous content business operations platform built on top of OpenClaw (an AI agent gateway). Each paying customer gets their own isolated Docker container running a team of AI agents that:


- Know their voice (and get better at it over time)
- Produce content autonomously (Mon-Fri daily drafts + weekly synthesis, running on cron jobs)
- Research their market (social listening across X, Reddit, Google SERPs)
- Manage their business operations (email, calendar, docs, tasks via Google Workspace)
- Improve themselves (log every win, error, and correction; promote patterns into permanent system improvements)
- Run 24/7 without the customer having to be present


The agents under the hood use Claude, GPT, and other LLMs via the OpenClaw gateway. Yes, it's "using Claude" at the atomic level. But what you get is not Claude. What you get is a persistent, learning, autonomous system that compounds intelligence about YOUR specific business over time.


The core insight: The skills are the atomic building blocks. But a skilled prompt engineer with those skills starts from zero every session. EchoOS starts from where it left off yesterday, with everything it learned from yesterday's corrections already baked into its instructions.

PART 6: THE VOICE SYSTEM (WHAT MAKES IT NOT JUST "A GOOD PROMPT")
========================================


THE PROBLEM WITH ONE-SHOT PROMPTS:
A prompt is loaded once, applied to the current task, and forgotten. The person's actual voice -- their rhythm, contradictions, proof points -- gets flattened into generic rules like "be conversational." If the agent makes a voice error, it's never captured. The next session repeats the same mistake. The person's actual examples are missing or generalized away.


WHAT THE VOICE SYSTEM DOES INSTEAD:


Layer 1: Identity Core (BASE-CRAFT.md + VOICE-PROFILE.md)
BASE-CRAFT.md (236 lines) is the universal foundation: story-first opening, em dash ban, receipts over claims, edit gate. VOICE-PROFILE.md (246 lines) is the person-specific identity built on top of that foundation.


VOICE-PROFILE.md contains 14 sections:
1. Root Identity -- who they are, in their own style
2. Identity Layers -- professional expertise, personal experience, philosophy, creativity, adversity, human texture
3. Core Tensions -- the contradictions that make them real (not flaws to fix, features to showcase)
4. Voice Characteristics -- what the voice IS and IS NOT, with concrete examples
5. Signature Patterns -- sentence rhythm, structural patterns, vocabulary fingerprint
6. Receipt Bank -- minimum 10 proof points with texture (names, numbers, scenes, before/after). This is the source of truth for what the agent can claim. If a proof point is missing, Echo flags the gap instead of inventing one.
7. Audience -- not demographics. The actual human struggling.
8. Worldview and Beliefs -- what they believe about how things work
9. Banned Patterns -- words, structures, tones never used
10. Platform Rules -- energy and rules per platform (LinkedIn vs Twitter vs email vs long-form)
11. Emotional Register -- calibration, humor style, vulnerability style
12. Writing Examples -- 3-5 excerpts from real writing that represent their voice at its best
13. Calibration Notes -- feedback from each review round, baked into the profile
14. Distortion Risks -- how the voice gets broken when pushed too far


Layer 2: Tactical Companion (SKILL-BRAND-VOICE.md)
Distills the Voice Profile into quick reference during drafting. 5-7 bullet points of voice DNA. Common substitutions table (banned word to replacement). Calibration check before submitting (6-point verification).


Layer 3: Routing System (VOICE-ARCHITECTURE.md)
6-layer model where layers override each other:
- Layer 1 (Identity Core): inviolable, almost never changes
- Layer 2 (Audience): quarterly or on strategic pivot
- Layer 3 (Tactical Voice Guide): monthly as voice evolves
- Layer 4 (Business/Offer): per offer launch
- Layer 5 (Edit Gate): as platforms change or style evolves
- Layer 6 (On-Call Deep Docs): loaded on demand for heavy reference work


VOICE-ROUTING.md tells the agent exactly which docs to load for which task type:
- Personal brand content: load Layers 1, 2, 3, 5
- Business/offer content: load Layers 1, 2, 4, 5
- Deep character work: also load Layer 6
- Session cache: if loaded in this session for same task type, reuse (don't reload)


Layer 4: Execution Enforcement (Edit Gate Checklist)
7-step mandatory checklist after every draft:
1. Scan for em dashes. Replace all.
2. Check opening. Does it start with a scene or thesis? If thesis, rewrite.
3. Count receipts. At least 2-3 specific moments, numbers, or contrasts? If not, add.
4. Read aloud. Does it sound like the person or a marketing team?
5. Check paragraph density. Any longer than 3 sentences? Break it up.
6. Check for guru language. Remove on sight.
7. Check close. Does it end in motion or vague inspiration? Fix it.


This is not "check if it looks good." It's mechanical, auditable enforcement.


Layer 5: Intake Protocol (INTAKE-PROTOCOL.md)
Phase 1: Collect 5+ pieces of person's own writing + 3 proof points with texture
Phase 2: Extract patterns from actual writing (sentence rhythm, vocabulary fingerprint, structural patterns)
Phase 3: Map identity layers from real examples
Phase 4: Discover banned patterns (words/structures/tones they never use)
Phase 5: Assemble into Voice Profile
Phase 6: Calibration Draft -- write one 300-500 word piece using the profile, present to the person, capture corrections. "The profile always looks good on paper. The first draft reveals whether it actually works."


Layer 6: Continuous Learning (CAIS)
Every voice correction is logged. When a pattern appears 3+ times, it's promoted to permanent config. Fast-promote mechanism means the config gets better every time the person gives feedback. Next session, the agent loads the updated config and the mistake doesn't repeat.


WHAT YOU CANNOT DO WITH ONE-SHOT PROMPT:
- Enforce that receipts are always specific (not generic claims)
- Require minimum 5 source materials before writing (a prompt just hopes they're there)
- Reject a draft that doesn't have proof points (a prompt can't enforce return-to-sender)
- Capture voice corrections and update the system next session (a prompt forgets)
- Route different task types to different voice doc stacks (a prompt loads once)
- Flag when a required voice doc is missing (a prompt hallucinates)
- Update the identity system based on calibration feedback (a prompt is static)

PART 7: CONTENT PIPELINE AND SEED SYSTEM
========================================


THE SEED SYSTEM:
Every content idea becomes a "seed." The seed is the atomic unit of content production.


5-Dimension Scoring:
1. Specificity -- how concrete is the idea? Generic = low. Specific scene/story = high.
2. Resonance -- how much does it echo the customer's voice/values? Random topic = low. Core identity = high.
3. Depth -- how many angles does it have? Single takeaway = low. 3+ angles = high.
4. Tension -- what's at stake? Purely positive/educational = low. Something real risked or gained = high.
5. Connectedness -- how does it relate to other seeds/content? Standalone = low. Threads into other work = high.


Seed Status Pipeline (continuous):
planted (raw idea captured) -> growing (Echo has enriched it, added dimensions) -> mature (rich enough, multiple angles identified) -> ripe (highest score, ready for drafting) -> harvested (published, archived)


CONTENT PRODUCTION WORKFLOW:


Daily (Mon-Fri):
1. Morning: Echo enriches all seeds (3x daily). Produces daily content readiness report.
2. Seed selection: Echo selects ripest seed (highest composite score across 5 dimensions + customer context)
3. Drafting: Echo writes standalone piece:
   - Applies VOICE-PROFILE.md (mandatory)
   - Story-first approach (scene + receipts, not claims)
   - Runs edit gate checklist (7 sweeps)
   - Self-grades on 8-point checklist
4. Delivery: Draft appears in drafts/ folder + Reports page notification
5. Customer review: Customer reviews at own pace. Approves or requests edits.
6. Approval: Moves to approved/ folder. Ready for publishing.


Weekly (Friday):
1. Synthesis piece: Echo reviews the week's 5 daily pieces
2. Throughline identification: "What's the theme running through this week?"
3. Long-form draft: Combines week's themes into single cohesive article/newsletter
   - Must work standalone (reader doesn't need the 5 dailies)
   - Same voice gate, edit gate, self-grade


Content Classification Model:
- Function: Demand (gets new attention), Trust (builds belief), Conversion (moves toward purchase), Identity (makes you a person, not a brand)
- Circle: Inner (core followers), Middle (engaged audience), Outer (cold/new audience)
- Awareness Level: Unaware > Problem Aware > Solution Aware > Product Aware > Most Aware


Weekly Content Balance Target:
- At least 1 piece from each content circle per week
- At least 1 Trust piece and 1 Demand piece per week
- Identity content develops naturally (don't force)

PART 14: SELF-IMPROVEMENT SYSTEM (CAIS)
========================================


CAIS = Continuous Agent Improvement System


PRE-TASK CHECKS (before any non-trivial task):
1. What am I assuming about this task?
2. What context might I be missing?
3. What could go wrong?
4. Have I loaded the right voice docs?


POST-TASK LOGGING (after completing any task):
- WINS.md: What worked well. What produced good output. What the human approved without edits.
- ERRORS.md: What went wrong. What was rejected. What broke a rule.
- LEARNINGS.md: What you now know that you didn't before. New patterns, preferences, context.
- VOICE-CORRECTIONS.md (tagged HIGH PRIORITY): Any voice or tone correction from the human. Logs: original text, corrected text, rule violated or gap revealed.
- ASSUMPTION-LOG.md: Assumptions that proved wrong. Reviewed periodically.


VOICE CORRECTION PROTOCOL (highest priority):
1. Log the correction with: original text, corrected text, what rule was violated
2. Check for gaps in VOICE-PROFILE.md or SKILL-BRAND-VOICE.md
3. If gap found: propose update via fast-promote mechanism
4. If "never do this" correction: add to banned patterns immediately


FAST-PROMOTE MECHANISM:
When a pattern appears 3+ times in post-task logs:
1. Draft a proposed change to the relevant config file
2. Present to the customer with evidence (the 3+ instances)
3. On approval: promote to permanent config


Promotion targets (in order of stability):
- TERROIR.md: fast-changing instance context. Promote here first.
- AGENTS.md: session protocol and hard rules. After 5+ recurrences with approval.
- SOUL.md: core voice rules. ALWAYS requires human approval. Never self-modify.
- VOICE-PROFILE.md: voice identity. ALWAYS requires human approval.


MID-SESSION CHECKPOINT (every 3-5 significant outputs):
1. Write current state to workspace-state.json
2. Confirm work is still aligned with session goal
3. Check that no non-owned scope has been absorbed
4. If voice drift detected: log it


IMPROVEMENT METRICS (tracked over time):
- Voice corrections per week (should decrease)
- Self-grade pass rate (should increase)
- Revision rounds per piece (should decrease)
- Fast-promote proposals: accepted vs rejected ratio
- Receipt Bank growth (should increase as more source material flows in)


THE INTELLIGENCE LOOP (end-to-end):
1. Pre-task: Agent asks "What am I assuming?" and loads voice docs
2. During task: Agent does the work
3. Self-check: Agent runs mandatory checklists
4. Post-task: Agent logs wins, errors, learnings, corrections, broken assumptions
5. Pattern detection: When a pattern appears 3+ times, it's flagged
6. Fast-promote: Human approves the pattern as a rule upgrade
7. Config update: Rule is baked into SOUL.md, AGENTS.md, VOICE-PROFILE.md, or TERROIR.md
8. Next session: Agent loads the updated config with the improved rules


The system gets better every week. A 90-day customer has a fundamentally different quality of output than a Day 1 customer.

PART 19: THE HONEST ANSWER
========================================


"Is this just a wrapper for something?"
At the atomic level, yes. Claude/GPT is under the hood. The individual skills use LLMs. A skilled prompt engineer with those skills CAN one-shot a piece of copy.


"What makes this different than trained Claude skills that one-shot low-ticket copy?"


1. PERSISTENCE: Skills start from zero every session. EchoOS starts from where it left off yesterday, with everything it learned from yesterday's corrections already baked into its instructions. A 90-day customer has a fundamentally different experience than a Day 1 customer.


2. AUTONOMY: Skills wait for you to prompt them. EchoOS runs 12 cron jobs that research, enrich, draft, analyze, and self-improve without being asked. Your content pipeline operates while you sleep.


3. COMPOUNDING: Skills produce a deliverable and it's done. EchoOS compounds value over time: seeds get richer, voice profiles get more accurate, the CAIS loop identifies and fixes weaknesses, context consolidation prevents drift. The system gets better at being YOUR system every week.


4. TEAM COORDINATION: Skills are individual prompts. EchoOS is 6 agents with distinct roles, guardrails, and hard rules that coordinate work: Zara routes, Echo writes, Sofi strategizes, Rex improves the system, Scout researches, Worker executes. The separation of concerns prevents the "one prompt trying to do everything" problem.


5. FULL-STACK OPERATIONS: Skills do one thing. EchoOS manages email, calendar, docs, ads, research, content, strategy, automation, and self-improvement as an integrated system. When Sofi notices your content mix doesn't match your goals, she tells Zara, who adjusts Echo's seed selection priorities. That's systems thinking, not prompting.


6. INFRASTRUCTURE: Skills run in your browser session. EchoOS runs in a dedicated Docker container with persistent storage, isolated data, and scheduled operations. It's not a tab you close. It's an always-on team.


The analogy: The skills are like having a recipe book and a really good kitchen. EchoOS is a restaurant with a head chef who knows your regulars, a sous chef who gets better every week, a maitre d' who manages the floor, and a kitchen that operates whether you're there or not. The recipe book is the same. The restaurant is the product.


For someone who needs an occasional piece of copy (one-shot, fire-and-forget), the skills alone are sufficient. EchoOS is overkill.


For someone who needs a content operation -- consistent output, voice consistency, strategic alignment, autonomous production, market intelligence, business analysis, and a system that gets smarter about their specific business over time -- that's what this is for.


The product is for people who need a content operation, not a content piece.
