#1 Differentiator

The Voice System

Not a "tone" slider. A 6-layer learning architecture that gets permanently better from every correction you make.

1

Identity Core

BASE-CRAFT.md (236 lines) + VOICE-PROFILE.md (246 lines). 14 sections: root identity, signature patterns, receipt bank, banned patterns, emotional register. Built from your actual writing and speaking.

2

Audience Context

Not demographics. The actual human struggling. Mapped from your words, not a form field.

3

Tactical Voice Guide

Quick reference during drafting. 5-7 voice DNA bullets. Common substitution table (banned word to replacement). Updated monthly as voice evolves.

4

Voice Routing System

Different doc stacks loaded for different task types. Personal brand vs. business/offer content vs. deep character work. Not one-size-fits-all.

5

Edit Gate (7 Mandatory Sweeps)

Every draft runs: em dash scan, scene-first check, receipt count, read-aloud test, paragraph density, guru language check, close quality. Mechanical. Auditable. Non-negotiable.

6

Continuous Learning (CAIS)

Every correction logged. Patterns appearing 3+ times trigger a proposed permanent update. You approve. It's baked in. That mistake doesn't happen again. Voice corrections decrease over time.

The Questions You Actually Have

Honest answers. No pitch-spinning.

"Is this just a wrapper for Claude or ChatGPT?"
At the atomic level, yes. LLMs are under the hood. Your car is "just" an engine with a frame around it. The real question is whether the system learns your voice across sessions, runs autonomously, coordinates six specialized agents, and improves from every correction. ChatGPT forgets you existed when you close the tab. EchoOS starts tomorrow from everything it learned today. That's not a wrapper. That's a different product category.
How is this different from Jasper, Copy.ai, or Poppy?
Jasper went enterprise. Copy.ai went enterprise. Both abandoned the SMB/creator market. Poppy is a great content creation tool for a session — it doesn't have persistent memory or autonomous operation. EchoOS is the product that category abandoned: a voice-matched, persistent, autonomous content team for solo operators doing $5K-$100K/mo. The key difference is what happens when you close the browser: Jasper, Copy.ai, and Poppy stop. EchoOS keeps running.
What if I don't like the drafts?
Correct them. That's the product working. Every correction you make gets logged by the CAIS system. When a pattern appears 3+ times — same mistake, same type of feedback — Echo proposes a permanent rule update. You approve, it's baked in. That specific voice error doesn't happen again. Week 1 drafts need more corrections than Week 4 drafts. The improvement is the product. If after 7 days the voice still misses, cancel before it bills. That's the deal.
How long until it sounds like me?
The voice system either hits or it doesn't on the first draft — that's the audition. If it misses, the corrections begin. Most users see meaningful improvement by Week 2-3 as corrections get logged and promoted. By Day 90, the system has absorbed hundreds of corrections, has a 14-dimension voice profile built from your actual content, and the error patterns that appeared early are permanent rules. Day 90 output is genuinely different from Day 1. That's not a promise — that's the mechanism.
What's the difference between EchoOS and BusinessOS?
EchoOS ($27/mo) is the content engine: Zara + Echo + Sofi (limited). Daily drafts, voice profiling, 6 cron jobs. If content consistency is your constraint, this is your product. BusinessOS ($97/mo) is the full team: all 6 agents, 12 cron jobs, Google Workspace automation (28 skills), weekly constraint check, Rex's self-improvement loop, Scout's market intelligence. If you're running a business and the bottleneck is operational capacity, not just content, BusinessOS is the right tier.
