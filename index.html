The Questions You Actually Have

The Questions You Actually Have

Honest answers. No pitch-spinning.

Is this just a wrapper for Claude or ChatGPT?
At the atomic level, yes. LLMs are under the hood. The real question is whether the system learns your voice across sessions, runs autonomously, coordinates six specialized agents, and improves from every correction you make. ChatGPT forgets you existed when you close the tab. EchoOS starts tomorrow from everything it learned today. That's not a wrapper. That's a different product category.
How is this different from Relevance AI, Poppy, or just using ChatGPT?
Relevance AI and Manus are powerful general-purpose agents, but they require you to build the workflows yourself. Poppy is a great canvas tool for a session, but it forgets you when you close the tab. EchoOS is an opinionated, pre-trained growth team. You aren't building agents from scratch. You're hiring a team that already knows your voice and compounds every session.
What if I don't like the drafts?
Correct them. That is the product working. Every correction gets logged. When a pattern appears 3+ times, Echo proposes a permanent rule update. You approve it, it is baked in. That specific voice error does not happen again. Week 1 drafts need more corrections than Week 4 drafts. The improvement is the product. If after 7 days the voice still misses, cancel before it bills. That is the deal.
How long until it sounds like me?
The voice system either hits or misses on the first draft. That is the audition. If it misses, the corrections begin. Most users see meaningful improvement by Week 2-3 as corrections get logged and promoted. By Day 90, the system has absorbed hundreds of corrections and has a 14-dimension voice profile built from your actual content. Day 90 output is genuinely different from Day 1. That is not a promise. That is the mechanism.
What's the difference between EchoOS and BusinessOS?
EchoOS ($27/mo) is the content engine: Zara + Echo + Sofi (limited). Daily drafts, voice profiling, Scout's market scans, Aria's pattern mapping. If content consistency is your constraint, this is your product. BusinessOS ($97/mo) is the full team: all 6 agents, 12 cron jobs, Google Workspace automation, weekly constraint check, Rex's self-improvement loop. If the bottleneck is operational capacity and not just content, BusinessOS is the right tier.
Can I cancel?

No retention emails. No annual lock-in unless you choose annual. Your data lives in your container for 30 days after cancellation so you can grab your voice profile and content before it is removed.
Do I need to be technical?
No. You chat with Zara. She handles everything. The 20-minute voice onboarding is a conversation, not a form. Google Workspace connection is guided and optional. The approval dashboard is read, approve, or correct. The technical depth is underneath. Docker containers, cron jobs, agent coordination. None of that is exposed to you as complexity. You are directing a team, not configuring software.
What happens to my data?
Every customer gets their own isolated Docker container. Your voice profile, content seeds, drafts, correction history, and business data are never shared with other customers and never used to train shared models. Your data is yours. The isolation is also what makes persistent voice learning possible. That is not just a privacy feature. That is the architecture that makes the product work.
