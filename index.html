The Questions You Actually Have

Honest answers. No pitch-spinning.

Is this just a wrapper for Claude or ChatGPT?
At the atomic level, yes. LLMs are under the hood. Your car is just an engine with a frame. The real question is whether the system learns your voice across sessions, runs autonomously, coordinates six specialized agents, and improves from every correction you make. ChatGPT forgets you existed when you close the tab. EchoOS starts tomorrow from everything it learned today. That's not a wrapper. That's a different product category.
How is this different from Jasper, Copy.ai, or Poppy?
Jasper went enterprise. Copy.ai went enterprise. Both abandoned the SMB and creator market. Poppy is a great content creation tool for a session. It does not have persistent memory or autonomous operation. EchoOS is the product that category abandoned: a voice-matched, persistent, autonomous content team for solo operators doing $5K-$100K/mo. The key difference is what happens when you close the browser. Jasper, Copy.ai, and Poppy stop. EchoOS keeps running.
What if I don't like the drafts?
Correct them. That is the product working. Every correction gets logged by the CAIS system. When a pattern appears 3+ times, Echo proposes a permanent rule update. You approve, it is baked in. That specific voice error does not happen again. Week 1 drafts need more corrections than Week 4 drafts. The improvement is the product. If after 7 days the voice still misses, cancel before it bills. That is the deal.
How long until it sounds like me?
The voice system either hits or misses on the first draft. That is the audition. If it misses, the corrections begin. Most users see meaningful improvement by Week 2-3 as corrections get logged and promoted. By Day 90, the system has absorbed hundreds of corrections and has a 14-dimension voice profile built from your actual content. Day 90 output is genuinely different from Day 1. That is not a promise. That is the mechanism.
What's the difference between EchoOS and BusinessOS?
EchoOS ($27/mo) is the content engine: Zara + Echo + Sofi (limited). Daily drafts, voice profiling, 6 cron jobs. If content consistency is your constraint, this is your product. BusinessOS ($97/mo) is the full team: all 6 agents, 12 cron jobs, Google Workspace automation (28 skills), weekly constraint check, Rex's self-improvement loop, Scout's market intelligence. If the bottleneck is operational capacity and not just content, BusinessOS is the right tier.
Can I cancel?
Yes, anytime, from your Whop dashboard. Cancel before the $7 trial converts and you pay nothing more. Cancel a monthly subscription before the next billing date and it stops. No save the sale flows. No guilt trip. No annual lock-in unless you choose annual. Your data lives in your container for 30 days after cancellation so you can grab your voice profile and content before it is removed.
Do I need to be technical?
No. You chat with Zara. She handles everything. The 20-minute voice onboarding is a conversation, not a form. Google Workspace connection is guided and optional. The approval dashboard is read, approve, or correct. The technical depth is underneath. Docker containers, cron jobs, agent coordination. None of that is exposed to you as complexity. You are directing a team, not configuring software.
What happens to my data?
Every customer gets their own isolated Docker container. Your voice profile, content seeds, drafts, correction history, and business data are never shared with other customers and never used to train shared models. Your data is yours. The isolation is also what makes persistent voice learning possible. That is not just a privacy feature. That is the architecture that makes the product work.
